Argos — Quant Trading Lab

Algorithmic trading systems with an anti-overfitting validation protocol

Overview

Most "profitable" backtests are statistical mirages without knowing it. Argos — named after Argos Panoptes, the hundred-eyed watchman of Greek myth — is a personal research project that built a validation protocol designed to kill those mirages: nothing slips past it, and only what survives the full protocol ever reaches execution with money (for now, simulated).

The study covers 7 years of market data (2019–2026), 180+ assets across US and European equities plus crypto, and tens of millions of price bars. Developed in collaboration with Claude (Anthropic) as a research, engineering and documentation copilot — direction, judgement and decisions by the author.

The Validation Protocol

Every candidate strategy goes through the same funnel, defined before looking at any results:

Pre-registered Hypothesis

Economic mechanism, falsifiable predictions and rejection criteria written down before the first number.

Cross-Universe Validation

Designed on asset group A, required to work on an untouched twin group B, then on a third universe downloaded on purpose.

One-Look Temporal Judge

The most recent data segment is consulted exactly once, at the very end — looking at it twice invalidates it.

Monte Carlo

Thousands of resamples to separate skill from luck — including a custom variant for compounding strategies.

Survivorship Audit

Re-tested after adding companies that actually went bankrupt to the universe — 2023 fallen banks included.

Forward Test

Nothing counts as validated until it runs for months in real time, automated on a broker's simulated account.

Results — 2 Survivors out of 9 Families

System 1 — US Equity Swing
Buys pullbacks within uptrends over a multi-day horizon, with a market-regime filter. Backtest 2021–2026 (friction included): CAGR ~21%, profit factor 1.25, max drawdown ~-34%. Passed 3 universes + the temporal judge; Monte Carlo probability of loss ~1%. Now in automated forward test.
System 2 — Crypto Trend
Buys strength breakouts 24/7, with a regime switch that turns the bot off during crypto winters. Backtest: CAGR ~60%, profit factor 1.85, max drawdown ~-29%. The twin universe performed better than the design one; Monte Carlo probability of loss ~0%. Now in automated forward test.

Historical simulation figures with pessimistic costs — not real returns, nor a promise of future returns. Drawdown is part of the price.

Hypotheses Buried with Data

The invisible work that gives value to the rest. Intraday trading (3 systems) died mathematically: a study on 69,000 observations showed the maximum signal available within the day is smaller than the cost of trading. Short selling (9 variants across 3 generations) was negative across the board — in the universe studied, weakness bounces, so selling dips equals buying squeezes. Momentum rotation on dividend stocks lost to its own passive equal-weight basket. And a system with spectacular design-group metrics (+9.9% per trade) turned out to be a statistical artifact that collapsed in both control universes — exactly what the protocol exists for.

Along the way, 7 methodology bugs were hunted down: results selected alphabetically instead of by date, standard Monte Carlo applied to compounded series ("impossible" -195% drawdowns), inflated CAGR denominators (caught through external review with another AI model), survivorship bias quantified at -6.5% instead of assumed, and paper leverage no real account would allow (Reg-T legal limit) with its hidden margin cost estimated.

Infrastructure

Python Backtesting Framework
Custom-built: pessimistic friction, look-ahead prevention, portfolio simulation, metrics (PF, CAGR, drawdown, deflated Sharpe).
Monte Carlo Suite
Bootstrap, permutation, drop and stress tests with charts — plus the custom compounding variant.
Autonomous Execution Bots
Run against a broker's REST API (simulated account): scheduled orders, broker-persisted stops, state reconciliation, unattended scheduling, portable across Windows/macOS.
Documentation
12+ technical reports (PDF/markdown) with a dated decision log — every change to every system has a date, a reason and a result.

Lessons Learned

Friction and cost matter more than signal — measure them first. If you don't pre-register the hypothesis, the backtest will tell you what you want to hear. Validation on virgin data isn't optional: it is the test. Losses arrive in clusters (regimes), and statistical medians underestimate the real pain. Burying 7 ideas with data is worth more than falling in love with 1 without it.
